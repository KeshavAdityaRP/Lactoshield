Commercialization

For producing our sachet system on a commercial scale,we had to give utmost importance to certain factors.

  •    Cost of one sachet.
  •    Shelflife of one sachet.
  •    Biocompatibility and toxicity of the sachet materials.
  •    Biodegradability of the sachet materials.

To elaborate on any of these factors,we had to study the sachet materials.

Discussions held with polymer chemists taught us that in the designing of any polymer material,certain aspects of the polymer 's chemistry should be taken into account.

This includes the polymer 's effect on environment, it's cost,melting and boiling point, how and when it can degrade and what are the byproducts, it's other physical properties like mechanical properties etc.

The outer membrane of the sachet will be made of 1kDa dialysis membrane. An alternative material is silk nanofiber filter.

The nanofibres are biodegradable and Biocompatibility. .though they are not widely available commercially.

The inner membrane will be made of polycaprolactone (PCL).This membrane had the following properties :

  •    Biocompatibility
  •    Biodegradable
  •    Melts at 60Â°C.
  •    Relatively cheap.

The sachet will be completely biodegradable and harmless in any way to animal cells.

The size of the sachet would be about the size of an average sized tea bag...although more studies have to be done on this matter.

Work is presently going on to reduce the cost of the sachet further.
