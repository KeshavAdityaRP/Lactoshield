Indian IGEM Meet Up

Meet ups are the perfect platforms to network, enhance and deepen the communication between fellow iGEMers. It’s a great warm up session before the Giant Jamboree where teams could present their projects to one another, share or swap ideas, enhance collaborative network and develop Human Practices.

Hence we SVCE_Chennai hosted the Indian iGEM meet up this year on the 23rd of July, inviting all the Indian teams participating in iGEM. We were joined by Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru and Indian Institute of Technology(IIT), Madras.

The session kicked off with an ice-breaker session following which our PI formally welcomed the gathering. This was then followed by the teams presenting their respective projects where each presentation was followed by questions from participants in other teams and professors in our department.

After this the visiting teams were given a tour of our department which was immediately followed by lunch. Following lunch teams deliberated on possible avenues for collaboration, both inside and outside the lab.

The meet up ended with a few fun games and new bonds formed between the teams.

Survey

Team Tec-Chihuaua

  •    Team SVCE_Chennai filled in the survey forms sent by the team Tec-Chihuaua which was based on both synthetic biology and their project to help them understand the view of our team members’ on the same.
  •    The intensity of milk spoilage issue in the Mexican region was analysed by our team with the aid of the Chihuaua team.(By circulating the forms in their country and gaining the responses of people belonging to their region).
  •    The team also filled in our survey forms which was intended to troubleshoot our problems with the lab techniques which is of great significance to us.

Team UPO Sevilla:.

  •    The issue of milk spoilage in the Spanish region was analysed by our team with the help of team UPO Sevilla. This helped us a lot to understand and analyse people’s opinion on a global issue.
  •    They also shared their views regarding troubleshooting lab techniques.
  •    We complemented them by circulating their forms on GMOs amongst our circles and acquired the people’s views on it. We prepared a cumulative report on it(which showed the awareness level on GMOs in India) and assisted them in their analysis.

Team Westminster_UoW

  •    The team aided us in framing the regulatory report for the United Kingdom region and also in getting opinion from the people of their country regarding the issue of milk spoilage in their region.
  •    And as a mutual help, we acquired the perspectives of the farmers in India regarding the usage of pesticides in their farms and their views on bio -based fertilizers as a potential alternative for the conventional chemical ones.

Team Aachen

  •    Team Aachen sent us their survey forms based on synthetic biology and people’s knowledge and we reciprocated them with our survey forms based on the issue of milk spoilage.
  •    Both the surveys were interchanged and the opinion of the people regarding the potency of the issues in the present scenario was obtained.

Team Purdue

  •    We assisted the team by putting about their survey regarding wastewater treatment among the people of India and obtaining their opinions on it.
  •    They helped us mutually by circulating the forms sent by us and aided us in the study of the issue in the U.S region.

Team Oxford, Team Edinburgh and Team Warwick, Team Kent and Team Technion

  •    These teams helped us by filling in our form regarding the troubleshooting of lab techniques.

Team Virginia

  •    We aided the team by circulating their survey forms based on Bio-containment and by gathering the opinions our team members on it.
